There's a tension between wanting to share your work and wanting it to be perfect before anyone sees it. I've been on both sides.

## The case for sharing early

When you share early, you get feedback you wouldn't get otherwise. People notice things you missed. They ask questions that reshape how you think about the problem.

## The uncomfortable part

It feels vulnerable. Half-baked ideas look half-baked. But that's the point — **the process is the product**, at least when it comes to learning.

## What I've found works

- Share the reasoning, not just the result
- Be honest about what you don't know
- Invite specific feedback, not general opinions

The best conversations I've had about my work started with something unfinished.
